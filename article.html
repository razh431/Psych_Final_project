Topics of Psychology

29 January 2021

All notes were taken out of lecture slides as well as the textbook. I've written a brief summary of each topic:

Altruism is prosocial behavior that benefits others who are suffering without regard to consequences for oneself, often at a personal cost. There are three types of altruistic behavior: social reward-- being praised by others, personal distress-- help others to reduce their own distress, and empathy-- identifying with someone.

Casual attribution is the process individuals used to explain his/her own and others’ behavior by linking an event to a cause. They are likely to infer that a personality trait is responsible for a behavior. However, these explanations can lead to consequences; for example, attributing a rejection from an internship could lead to a lack of ability leads to unhappiness and withdrawal.

Compliance is responding favorably to an explicit request by another person. “door in the face” compliance approach that involves asking for a first favor that is large and unreasonable, followed by a request for a smaller favor, since it’s seen as a concession. Foot-in-the-door technique is compliance that involves an initial small request that most people comply with, following up with larger request involving real behavior of interest

Culture can often influence thought and norms. In independent cultures, people tend to think of themselves as distinct social entities, tied to each other by voluntary bonds. In interdependent (collectivistic) cultures, people are more likely to define themselves as part of a collective, placing less importance on individual freedom or personal control over their lives. East Asian cultures are more interdependent whereas the West encourages more independent cultures.

Effort Justification is one of the paradigms of Cognitive dissonance. Many times we engage in a lot of effort for something but it may look disappointing. In other words, the more efforts and sacrifices someone puts in to pursue a goal, the more attractive the goal seems.

On the topic of attraction, emotional attribution is the theory that nature of arousal can be misattributed to another emotion, so the emotion can be diminished, enhanced, or changed. Both positive and negative emotions can be attributed. The attachment theory states that there are three types of attachment styles, which are formed by how their parents gave attention to them.

Sense of Self is defined as the way a person views his or her traits and beliefs. It covers both the 'inner' and 'outer' self. A sense of self refers to the self being a social entity that is forged largely through interactions with others

There are two types of social norms: the descriptive norm, which is behavior exhibited by most people in a given context, and the prescriptive (injunctive) norm, which is the way a person is supposed to behave in a given context. Conformity is changing one’s behavior or beliefs in response to some real or perceived pressure from others, sometimes to follow a social norm.
