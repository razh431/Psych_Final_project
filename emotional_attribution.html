Emotional Attribution

14 February 2021

Fall semester is usually when people scout for summer internships. Well, maybe just in my friend group-- most of my friends are CS majors. Unfortunately, I’m still applying and interviewing. As a girl in STEM, it’s hard to attribute this failure to lack of ability since I’ve applied to so many with minimal results.

The rejection that stung the most was after passing 2 technical interviews and was rejected after the personality interview. I’m sure all the candidates all had different personalities, but logically, shouldn’t every try to act very similar? I was very professional, very eager and excited when I was being interviewed, but maybe there was just a more qualified candidate.

I was consoled by friends that “I’ll get a better company” or that I deserve much better, but my time will come. Despite their efforts, I don’t think I feel much better. I think they believe in a just world, but I am more upset by the fact that I was so close to getting that internship that it made this rejection hurt a lot more.

If only I had worked harder. If I practiced my behavioral questions more, if I said something differently in the interview, if I had researched the company more, I may have gotten the internship. Counterfactual thinking may be unproductive, but it’s helped me be more determined I guess.

Rachel
