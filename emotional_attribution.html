Emotional Attribution

14 February 2021

It’s Valentine’s Day, and I would really like to appreciate Boyfriend. When my boyfriend and I met, I never thought we would start dating. After running into him in the research lab, same major and year, I guess due to the mere exposure effect, we became familiar with each other. Our friendship developed into a relationship after a hike on the Cornell suspension bridge. Maybe due to heights, maybe because of him, I suddenly felt attraction towards him, and I guess he became my boyfriend shortly after.

Boyfriend is rather avoidant in our relationship while I am the opposite. When we started dating, I wouldn’t mind hanging out more with him but he seems to need more space. I had lots of trouble understanding his perspective-- if he really likes me, shouldn’t he want to hang out with me more? I think Boyfriend seems to fear emotional intimacy; maybe his parents did not seem to give him enough time and attention-- they were doctors.

I think Boyfriend has learned to provide a sense of security and commitment to me. I’m aware of the three factors that influence commitment, and he has exceeded all 3. I never compare Boyfriend to alternatives, am satisfied, and invested into this relationship. Commitment is linked to longer relationships because it increases relationship-promoting behaviors like forgiveness, self-sacrifice.

Rachel
