Compliance

22 April 2021

I, unfortunately, needed friends to buy tickets for a raffle today. I hate asking friends for help on fundraisers-- most friends seemed to be “guilt-tripped” into buying them. They’re altruistic because I’m their friend and they want to help, not because they actually care about the prizes.

But it had to be done. I shamelessly asked them to buy 100 tickets, which they rejected immediately, but offered to buy 1 or 2 instead. I laughed and told them they fell for the “foot out the door” technique, where I demand a large favor to ask for a small one instead. They told me to be a marketing major instead. I ended up selling almost all of the tickets in 1 day. I told them they can ask me to buy them anytime-- after all, the norm of reciprocity applies even in friendships and selling raffle tickets!

Rachel
